the idea

A photo goes in, a paintable kit comes out. The brand should feel like a warm friend who hands you the fun part.

hello paint turns a customer's photo into a custom paint-by-number kit they print and paint at home. The personality is a cozy cool girl: warm, cheery, friendly to all ages, and unmistakably made by a person. The look is flat and hand-painted, never techy or arcade. The palette leads with warm cream and a raspberry "hello," with teal and sunshine doing the eye-catching, and it stays clear of orange on purpose, so the brand reads as human-made.

the smile

We dropped the waving hand for a single, happy smile.

Every attempt at a literal hand felt busy or stiff: too many fingers, too much to read at sticker size. So the third mark became what the brand was reaching for all along: a warm, open smile. One raspberry curve, a teal streak of motion, and a sunshine wink: it reads in an instant, paints in a single colour, and feels exactly like a friendly hello.

typography

Fraunces: headings

Optical serif, weight 600 (and 500 italic for taglines). Warm and editorial. One headline per view; never set long body copy in it.

Nunito Sans: body & wordmark

Friendly sans. 400 / 700 for text, 800 for the wordmark and kit numbers. Lowercase wordmark, one space, always.

voice & tone

Cozy cool girl. Plain over clever. Invite, don't instruct.

Talk the way you'd hype a friend. Lead with the before and after. Ask for the worst pet photo. Keep the handle lowercase. Show the transformation, not the process.

do & don't

Do

  • Keep "hello" raspberry and "paint" plum ink.
  • Lead layouts with cream so the brights pop.
  • Use the one-colour smile for stickers and labels.
  • Pair the snapshot logo with a real before & after.
  • Give every mark its clear space.

Don't

  • Add orange or terracotta anywhere.
  • Capitalize or close up the wordmark.
  • Add gradients, shadows, or arcade styling.
  • Set body text in bright raspberry or teal.
  • Stretch, rotate, or recolor the marks.
The redesign: an earlier waving-hand mark was replaced with the simple smile above, and the before-and-after logo now reads 1 · 2 · 3 with the sun sitting in the same spot in both the photo and the numbered halves. Everything else (palette, type, and the no-orange rule) is unchanged.
