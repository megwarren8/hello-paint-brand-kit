§1 · how etsy decides who rises

etsy search is not a mystery, it’s a habit loop. knowing the loop tells you where effort pays.

the loop, in plain words
read once, then act on §2, §6
etsy ranks a listing by how well it converts the people who see it: clicks, favourites, and above all purchases and five-star reviews. that means rank follows quality, compounding slowly.

what you control directly:
• the words, title and tags decide which searches you appear in at all (§2)
• the first photo, it alone decides whether a scroller clicks (§3)
• the number of doors, every listing is its own entry in search; three niche listings beat one general one (§4)
• the timing, gift searches spike on a calendar you can see coming (§5)
• the reviews, the review ask in the copy vault (§6) is the single highest-leverage message in the system

what to ignore: daily renewing, tag-stuffing, follower counts. they’re noise next to one more five-star photo review.

how the tags were chosen (so future edits stay sharp)
reference, no pasting needed
• every tag is a phrase a buyer would actually type, “custom pet portrait”, not “bespoke artwork”
• the title’s opening words (“custom paint by number kit from your photo”) already rank, tags add the searches the title can’t fit: gifts, occasions, recipients
• occasion + recipient tags (“gift for mom”, “anniversary gift”) catch gift-buyers, who are most of etsy
• don’t repeat a word combination the title already has in the same order, spend the characters on new searches
• revisit twice a year: swap the weakest tag for whatever buyers keep writing in messages

one honest rule for spin-offs
before you list
each spin-off needs its own first photo showing that subject, a pet before & after on the pet listing, a wedding one on the wedding listing. the listing template in the library takes any photo pair. don’t launch a spin-off until you have one real example of that subject to show; borrowed enthusiasm reads as stock.

a note on pet memorials
read before posting anything in this lane
pet memorial buyers find you year-round, there’s no season for missing a dog. never run a “promotion” here. the pet spin-off listing and the gentle tags do the finding; your warm photo-check message (copy vault §6) does the rest. this lane grows on care, not campaigns.
§6 · the monthly once-over

fifteen minutes on the first friday of the month, after the weekly post. this is the whole maintenance plan.

the once-over, four checks
first friday of the month · etsy → shop manager
copy
1. stats → search terms: which searches found you last month? if buyers keep arriving through a phrase that isn’t a tag yet, make it one, swap out the weakest tag (§2).
2. photos: did a customer send a great finished painting? watermark it, and consider it for slot 2 or the review card in slot 9 (§3).
3. reviews: every new review gets a reply within the month, the reply bank is in copy vault §7.
4. occasions: look six weeks ahead on the gifting calendar (§5). if a window is coming, put the post in the queue now.
